<ol>
  <li>Our Assessment Policy comprises  a learner-centred and balanced assessment (formative and summative) system that  supports quality teaching and learning, builds intrinsic motivation in students to learn,  and develops in them the capacity to learn for life.
    <ol>
      <li type="a">Formative Assessment (e.g.,  daily class work, quizzes, homework, etc.)  plays an important role in informing students about their learning  progress and in identifying learning  gaps to address. Students’ performance in Formative Assessment also helps teachers evaluate the students’ readiness  for Summative Assessment.</li>
      <li type="a">Summative  Assessment (e.g., Weighted Assessments (WAs) and End-of-Year Examination (EYE))  are milestone checks on students’ learning progress. It also evaluates their readiness  for progression to the next level of learning. <br>
        <br>
      </li>
    </ol>
  </li>

<p>Results of  the preliminary examinations will be taken on its own<br>
Assessments  are important milestone checks on students’ learning. Students are expected to  attend and complete all assessments, unless they are excused with valid  reasons.</p>
<ol start="3">
  <li>In terms of Assessment Design,  besides pen-and-paper WAs, each subject (except coursework-based subjects, for  e.g., AEP, D&amp;T and FCE) will have up to one Graded Assignment (GA) per year that will be  considered as a WA, i.e., for only WA1, WA2 or WA3, for Sec/Year 1 – 3 and Year  4. Each GA will have a maximum duration of 3 weeks (if 10% weighting) or 4 weeks (if 15% weighting) – students will be advised  on the number of hours to be spent on each GA. Each GA will be completed  within the term.</li>
  <li>The table below provides an  update to our Summative Assessment Practices:</li>
</ol>
<table cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0" align="center" border="1">
  <tbody><tr>
    <td valign="top" width="151" style="border-style:solid;border-width:1px"><p>Absent from a Weighted Assessment (pen-and- paper test)</p></td>
    <td valign="top" width="416" style="border-style:solid;border-width:1px"><ul>
      <li>The student is required to produce a Medical Certificate (MC) or provide a valid reason (e.g., MOE- approved competition) that is approved by the school management.</li>
      <li>‘VR’, which stands for “Valid Reasons”, will be indicated for the subject    test score on the result slip.</li>
      <li>The student is required to sit for    the test as soon as he can    after returning to school. The    assessment script will be    graded but the marks will not count towards summative assessment. The scores    will only be used    as reference for    promotion and/or choice    of subject combination.</li>
      <li>If the student is absent from the WA without a valid    reason, zero mark will be awarded.</li>
    </ul></td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td valign="top" width="151" style="border-style:solid;border-width:1px"><p>Missing the submission deadline of Weighted    Assessment (Graded Assignment)</p></td>
    <td valign="top" width="416" style="border-style:solid;border-width:1px"><ul>
      <li>The student is required to produce an MC or provide    a valid reason that is approved by the school management.</li>
      <li>The student is required to submit    the assignment as soon as he returns to school and 100% of his marks    will be awarded.</li>
      <li>20% of the student’s marks will be forfeited for    every working day that he submits his GA late.</li>
      <li>Zero mark will be awarded if the student    is late for more than 4 working days without a    valid reason.</li>
    </ul></td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td valign="top" width="151" style="border-style:solid;border-width:1px"><p>Absent from End-of-Year Examination</p></td>
    <td valign="top" width="416" style="border-style:solid;border-width:1px"><ul>
      <li>The student is required to produce an MC or provide    a valid reason that is approved by the school management.</li>
      <li>The student is required to sit for    the examination as soon as he can after returning to school. The examination    script will be graded but the marks will not count towards summative assessment. The scores will only be used    as reference for    promotion and/or choice    of subject combination.</li>
      <li>If the student is absent from the    examination without a valid reason    such as having    an MC, zero mark will be    awarded.</li>
    </ul></td>

<p>For Sec/Year 2 HMTL students  who did not meet the requirement of a minimum grade of D7 with their  Sec/Year 2 HMTL Overall results, they would have to offer MTL instead of HMTL  in Sec/Year 3.</p>
